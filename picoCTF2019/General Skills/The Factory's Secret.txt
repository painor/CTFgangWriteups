This challenge is more of a chance to the game interface. I find this to be the most enjoyable aspect of playing picoCTF.
Th ere are various glyphs (6) hidden around the game that the player should collect.
The first thing you may notice is an advert for fish oil below the game. The ad says fish oil provides VPN and IDS. Seems fishy.
Save this file, we'll get back to it later. 

* When you enter the general skills room, you'll notice that there is a persistent beeping on one side of the room.
What's that? Morse!
I used a handy morse audio decoder app (Morse Code Reader and Recorder - G0HYN RX Morse by Robbie Robertson on Android) to decode the message.
This message talks about pillars and gives a sequence.
This is referring to the pillars with switches in the Reverse Engineering room. Go get that glyph.

* The next glyph you can find is in the binary exploitation room. Binary exploitation is often about buffer overflows.
Think of the two doors in the background as switches that you flip to 1 by going through them. Try to overflow the 00.
If you're on the right track, the background starts to change faster and the background music speeds up. A yellow door appears. 
Go through it and collect your glyph.
 
* The web exploitation glyph is reachable through a hidden tunnel that you get to by going through the first (obvious) tunnel.
Just keep trying parts of the wall until you reach it.

* Cryptography. There should be a dialog option at some point that tells you:
"why should you open a jpg with a text editor? Something's fishy!"
This dialog will also include a little ASCII fish like the one in the advert.
Open the advert using a text editor, and you'll see something about a grave plot that should be secured.
I found it by chance, just by passing through all the graves.

* There wasn't really a trick to the forensics glyph or general skills glyphs.

Once you have all six glyphs, you will obtain a QR code. The QR code reveals a password. Hmm.. where would we need that?
Go to the computer where you initially woke up and input the password. Then you get to overhear an interesting conversation. . .
